There's a lot to learn if you're trying to learn how to play or watch League of Legends. Let's break it down.

Roles:

There are 5 roles in League of Legends. Take a quick look at the map Summoner's Rift in League of Legends so you can better understand where each role goes. "Map of Summoner's Rift" Each role has strengths and weaknesses. I will list them below.

Top lane

The top laner the champion that earns its gold and experience by farming the enemy minions that come marching down the top lane. Top lane is the most independent role in the game. For the most part, you're on your own. Top laners usually take the Teleport summoner spell so they can join a teamfight on the other side of the map. Typically, top laners choose champions with good sustain so they can remain in the top lane without having to recall for extended periods of time.

Strengths:

  • Because the top laner is alone in the top lane, they doesn't have to share any of their gold or experience. This means they can easily become the highest level champion on the team.
  • Top laners usually have pretty linear scaling so they're strong early, mid, and late game.

Weaknesses:

  • The top and bot lanes are long lanes and unlike the bot laner, the top laner is alone in its long lane. It's dangerous to be alone in a long lane so top laners are highly susceptible to ganks by the enemy jungler.
  • Top laners can get counter-picked. There are certain champion matchups that favor one champion in lane. This means the top laner can be zoned off their minions or risk being killed in an all in.
  • While top laners can safely collect all the experience available to them, it isn't as easy for them to collect all the gold from the minions due their lack of ranged waveclear.

Jungle

The jungler is the champion that earns its gold and experience by clearing the neutral jungle camps that spawn in the jungle between lanes. The jungler is the brains behind the operation. They have the most decision making to do. While laners are focused on their lanes, the jungler is deciding when and where to:

  1. Clear their jungle camps.
  2. Counter jungle the enemy jungle camps.
  3. Gank enemy laners.
  4. Take objectives such as the Dragon, Rift Herald, Baron Nashor, or enemy structures.
  5. Getting vision down to track the enemy jungler.

Strengths:

  • Clearing the jungle is safer than laning because jungle camps always do the same thing and there are no enemy champions nearby.
  • The jungler spends most of their time in the enemy's fog of war. This gives them the element of surprise when they show up turning a losing 1v1 into a winning 2v1.
  • Junglers take the summoner spell Smite which deals a large amount of true damage to minions or monsters. This makes it so they can nearly guarantee the last hit on monsters so they aren't stolen by the enemy team. They can also use smite to steal monsters from the enemy team in a smite-off.

Weaknesses:

  • Junglers take more blame from their teammates than any other role when things go wrong.
  • It's both a blessing and a curse how influential a jungler's decisions are. Poor decisions can be hugely costly. Failing a gank can turn a winning lane into a losing lane.

Mid lane

Similarly to the top laner, the mid laner is the champion that earns its gold and experience by farming the enemy minions that march down the mid lane. The mid lane is the shortest lane in the game. This also makes it the safest lane because the mid laner never has to venture far from the saftey of their tower. Mid laners are typically powerful champions with good ranged waveclear. They clear their minion wave and then have time to leave their lane and return before their tower is in danger.

Strengths:

  • Mid laners earn just as much experience as the top laner and as much gold as the bot laner.
  • Because they're in the middle of the map, after pushing their wave, the mid laner can roam anywhere else on the map.

Weaknesses:

  • The enemy mid tower is the hardest tower to take because it is the heaviestly defended tower.
  • The mid laner is a solo laner and as such is vulnerable to being 1v2'd.

Bot lane

The bot laner is the champion that earns its gold and experience by farming the enemy minions that come marching down the bot lane. The bot laner is different than the top and mid laners in that it isn't alone in the bot lane. The bot lane is a duo lane which is occupied by both the bot laner and their support. While the bot laner gets all the gold from farming the minions, it splits the experience earned by the minions with their support.

Strengths:

  • The bot laner has a support that is there to keep them safe while they last-hit every minion. Because of this, the bot laner should earn the most gold on the team.
  • Bot laners scale with items better than any champion in the game. This means they become the strongest member of the team come lategame.

Weaknesses:

  • Because bot laners scale so well with items, they're really weak until they have items. That's why they're the ones with the support. The support is to cover their earlygame weakness.
  • The bot laner shares their minion experience with their support. This means the bot laner and the support are the lowest leveled champions in the game and often times the squishiest.

Support

The support is the other champion that earns its gold and experience in the bot lane, but it doesn't earn gold by killing minions - the minion gold in the bot lane goes to the bot laner but the experience from the minions is split between the two. So where does the support get gold from? Well supports get to start the game with a special class of starting item from the item shop that is only viable on supports. This is called a support item and they have 1 of 2 passives that allow them to earn gold:

  • Tribute: Damaging spells and attacks against champions or buildings grant 15 gold. This can occur up to 3 times every 30 seconds.
  • Spoils of War: While nearby an ally, basic attacks execute minions below 50% of their max Health (30% for ranged) and grants them kill gold. (These effects recharge every 45 seconds. Max 3 charges.)

The support's purpose is to keep their bot laner safe throughout the early game. They're also tasked with vision control along with the jungler. They're always expected to have wards on hand.

Strengths:

  • Freedom. The support doesn't have to last hit minions so they can put their focus on other things. So long as their bot laner is safe, they can focus on damaging enemy champions, getting vision down, roam mid lane, or group up with their jungler to make a play.
  • The support can be a playmaker. Supports are usually champions that don't need items to be effective. Their kits bring utility or crowd control to their team.

Weaknesses:

  • The support is the poorest champion in the game. There aren't any minions or jungle monsters for them to last hit.
  • The support is the lowest leveled champion in the game. They share experience with their bot laner, but they often times miss experience when they leave the lane.
